üõ† Efficiency Tips for Teams

‚Ä¢ Use transcripts and meeting summaries as input to AI for auto-drafting mails/action items

‚Ä¢ Switch between models based on task type: e.g., Claude Sonnet for query optimization, GPT-4o for formatting/content polishing, O3 for structured thinking/reasoning

‚Ä¢ Create reusable AI prompts/templates for recurring tasks. The models are natively not capable of giving critical feedback. If you give an idea, by-default it assumes it is a good idea. If you want critical feedback you are supposed to ask. Keep in mind that AI models tend to be slightly sycophantic on the first pass‚Äîthey often assume the original structure or approach is correct and only make minor tweaks when asked to "improve" something. To get true value, you need to explicitly prompt them to challenge the structure itself. For example: ‚ÄúNow ignore how I‚Äôve framed this and use your own judgment to redesign or restructure it as you see fit.‚Äù This encourages the model to think independently rather than just polish the surface.
